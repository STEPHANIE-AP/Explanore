1. Proxima Centauri b

Proxima Centauri b is an Earth-sized exoplanet located in the habitable zone of the red dwarf star Proxima Centauri, roughly 4.2 light-years away. Discovered in 2016, it has a mass about 17% greater than Earth's and completes an orbit every 11.2 days.

2. TRAPPIST-1e

TRAPPIST-1e is one of seven Earth-sized planets orbiting the ultra-cool dwarf star TRAPPIST-1, located about 39 light-years from Earth. Its potential for habitability makes it a significant target for future exploration.

3. Kepler-186f

Kepler-186f is the first Earth-sized exoplanet discovered in the habitable zone of another star, located about 500 light-years away. It offers an exciting opportunity for the study of life beyond our solar system.

4. Luyten b

Luyten b is a super-Earth located around 12.2 light-years from Earth in the habitable zone of the red dwarf star Luyten's Star. Its proximity makes it a prime candidate for further study.

5. Ross 128 b

Ross 128 b is an Earth-sized exoplanet orbiting the nearby red dwarf star Ross 128, approximately 11 light-years away. Its potential for habitability makes it an interesting target for astronomers.

6. Gliese 581g

Gliese 581g is a super-Earth located in the habitable zone of the red dwarf star Gliese 581, about 20 light-years away. It is a key candidate for the search for extraterrestrial life.

7. Teegarden b

Teegarden b is an Earth-sized exoplanet orbiting the M-type star Teegarden's Star, located around 12.5 light-years from Earth. Its location in the habitable zone adds to its intrigue.

8. Kepler-442b

Kepler-442b is a super-Earth located approximately 1,200 light-years away in the constellation Lyra. Its Earth-like qualities make it an interesting subject for study.

9. K2-72e

K2-72e is an Earth-sized exoplanet located about 245 light-years away in the habitable zone of its star, K2-72. Its characteristics may provide insights into planetary formation.

10. WASP-121b

WASP-121b is a hot Jupiter located approximately 850 light-years away in the constellation Puppis. Its extreme conditions make it a valuable target for atmospheric studies.

11. HD 209458b

HD 209458b, also known as Osiris, is a hot Jupiter located about 159 light-years away. Its atmosphere has been studied in detail, revealing interesting properties.

12. HD 189733b

HD 189733b is another hot Jupiter located approximately 64 light-years away. Its extreme weather conditions make it a fascinating subject for research.
